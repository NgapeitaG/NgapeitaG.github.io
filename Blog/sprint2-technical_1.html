In this blog entry, I am going to teach you the difference between a margin, border and the padding of an element.

What an element is: Headings, paragraphs, text, images etc

You do not have to be a super-talented computer hacker to understand computer terms so this page breaks down three simple terms for you.



Margin

A margin is the space outside of something. In my own words, it is the space outside of a border.


Padding

Padding is the space inside something.

Padding adds space between the text and border on the inside of the border.







Border

Borders are the outlines of an element. They are boxes which can emphasise the text inside of it. They can indicate if a piece of text is a heading or you can use borders to separate certain elements to the rest of your elements on your page.

They can be solid lines, dashed, dotted, double, grooved, ridged, inset, outset and hidden.

Border-style

To make a border around an element, all you need is border-style. Like I said before, these values can be solid, dotted, dashed, double, groove, ridge, inset, outset and hidden.

Border-colour
